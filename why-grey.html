Our manifesto

We
believe
this.

I

The grey zone is
where mastery
happens.

Nobody becomes a builder in the extremes. Not in theory (white), not in failure (black). The grey zone is where you solder a joint for the fifteenth time until it's right. That's our zone. We live there permanently.

II

A drone that
looks good
isn't enough.

We've seen too many builds that photograph beautifully and fly for three minutes. We care about what happens after takeoff — centre of gravity, motor temperature, vibration harmonics, flight time. The unglamorous stuff that actually matters.

III

Small is
a feature,
not a bug.

We're not a warehouse. We don't have a ticket system or a support bot. When you message GreyProps, the person who replies is the same person who built the thing you're asking about. That's the point.

IV

Teach it.
Don't just
sell it.

Our workshops exist because we hate the idea of someone flying a machine they don't understand. A build you assembled yourself, with your own hands, is a build you can fix. Knowledge matters more than the box it came in.
